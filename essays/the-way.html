# change

Getting into IIT Delhi was a ray of hope for me, however my journey here hasn't started off on a high note. I am still carrying some emotional baggage from the three-year long "Bataan Death March". And the environment at [IIT] Delhi is providing additional challenges, that I did not anticipate.

What kind of emotional baggage, you say? Well, here we go... three years of isolation coupled with severe clinical depression has changed me, here's a diff of the two versions of my mind.

... seems like a pretty heavy diff, doesn't it?
# indulgence (pre-depression) indulgence (post-depression)
1 Religiously listen to romantic and sad songs I'm no longer a hopeless romantic!
2 Sci-tech documentaries captured my imagination (I would spend days on end thinking about climate change and how technology might help solve it...) Documentaries have gotten boring. They all seem the same, recyling the same content over and over... (or maybe the world just isn't moving fast-enough for me!)
3 Captivated by Sci-fi movies (I used to want Star Wars to be a reality... hopping across planets, across solar-systems, now that's the way to live!) I don't find movies (of anykind) captivating. It feels as if they no longer add any value to me; they don't resonate with me anymore!
4 Loved watching tear-jerker movies (Dead Poets Society, October Sky, Good Will Hunting, The Great Gatsby,... and on & on) Stopped watching movies all-together (doesn't feel like a worthy use of my time)
5 Loved getting immersed into a video-game's campaign, as if I was living it (mourning Ghost's death and seeking revenge for Shepard's betrayel, strategizing to capture the Cherbourg Harbour, countering the Ceph all the while enhancing my nanosuit... Oh, I've been alive for so long!) Don't feel like playing games anymore (although my laptop's lack of support for modern titles might have something to do with this!)
6 Always found of idea of me being a badass nerd quiet fascinating (Tony Stark my role model... Genius, Billionare, Playboy, Philanthropist!). Everyting I did, everything I learned, I did it with the express intent of achieveing it; but somehow it always seemed far away! I don't want to think it anymore, I want to be it.

Although, it seems like I've lost a lot, the truth is - I've gained everything and lost nothing! What do I mean by that? I spent a lot of time, often days on end, thinking about all the ways life could turn-out. And in retrospect, all I did was... think! And with all the purposeless indulgences out of the way, I have gained time, which I can now use to move towards my purpose. What purpose? I don't want to think it, I want to do it - and this is where my vocation comes into the picture!
