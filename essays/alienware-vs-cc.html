# backstory

I'm currently using a 2016 Dell Inspiron i7559 laptop. Although, this wasn't the best even in its time, it was decent for light-gaming and video-editing (which is what I used to do at the time), especially given my budget. Now, it has been six years since I purchased it, and the laptop has transmuted into a desktop. Don't worry the univese is intact, I haven't broken any laws of physics (yet!) - the laptop's battery died about four months ago, and I had to remove it since it was on the verge of exploding; the laptop's soft-touch plastic surface, and rubber support-pads have deteriorate. I had to peel off most of it since stickiness was getting annoying, to say the least; the ports have gotten loose, just touch the HDMI port and my monitor goes blank; and on & on...

In fact, it is so old (atleast by modern standards) that Dell's own website refuses to acknowledge that this laptop ever existed! So, it seems like a worthy time to upgrade to a new laptop.

# the conundrum

My hectic schedule doesn't allow me to play video-games (I must admit, a part of me is dying to play COH-3). And I'm currently working on machine learning, and the need of the hour are GPU's - as many as possible. So, it would be wise of me to spend the money, that I would otherwise spend on the Alienware (the thing costs an eye-watering $4,894.45 USD!), to buy a cloud-computing subscription. Easy, right?... NO!

Besides, uncle Teddy knocking through my conscience, there is a part of that always wanted an Alienware... I mean who wouldn't, just look at the damn thing; its power and pretty all packed in one.
